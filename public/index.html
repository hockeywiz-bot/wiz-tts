Björck Set to Anchor Jets' Second Line as Rookie

By HockeyWiz Staff — Updated moments ago

Viggo Björck is leaving the SHL to pursue NHL opportunities with the Winnipeg Jets. He shared his plans with Djurgården late last week, receiving their blessing, and is expected to sign his entry-level contract with Winnipeg shortly.

(Photo by Colby Spence/Illegal Curve)

Björck is not only expected to make the Jets' roster. He's going to be given every opportunity to start the season as Winnipeg's second-line centre. Winnipeg's commitment to the 18-year-old is nearly unprecedented, reflecting a partial shift in Jets philosophy and extreme belief in Björck's NHL ability.

Will it be worth it? And why do the Jets believe Björck is ready for the opportunity?

GP: 42 | G: 6 | A: 9

To answer these questions, one must understand the scope of the challenge ahead of him. Björck is a tremendous prospect with elite hockey sense, but his NHL transition will come with growing pains. The NHL's 84-game schedule will be more physically demanding than anything he's experienced before. His opponents will be bigger, stronger, and faster than what he's used to in the SHL. And the game itself will be different.
